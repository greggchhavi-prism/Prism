The map

There are many ways to hold that map.

  • How you see yourself when you're honest and quiet.
  • How you show up when the pressure is on.
  • How you move through the world on an ordinary day.
  • How the people closest to you would describe you if asked.

Each of these is real. Each tells a different part of the story.

Prism maps one specific lens — how you receive and process the world from the inside. One piece of a larger picture. A place to start.

Every Prism profile was built from the pattern logic first — then checked against the documented lives of real people before anyone ever saw it. Where the map didn't fit, we changed the map.

Why Prism exists

Prism was built at a kitchen table, by a family, for the person it started with.

It started with one map. Then it fit someone else. Then it became clear it could fit anyone — because we all have superpowers and shadow sides. Every single one of us.

"Prism doesn't promise easy. It promises that when you understand how you work, you can be present for all of it — the hard and the extraordinary both."

What knowing does

Luck is preparation meeting opportunity.

Knowing how your brain works — how you process, what costs you, what you're quietly brilliant at — means you show up prepared. Not just for the moments you plan for. For the ones you don't.

The map doesn't create the opportunity. It makes sure you're ready when it arrives.

Knowing your wiring prepares you. But it does something else too — it gives you language for who you are. Language you can share. And when two people can both say this is how I work, something real becomes possible. That's where trust starts. And trust is where results come from.

What Prism is not

You've probably taken a personality assessment before.

Tools like Myers-Briggs, DISC, and the Enneagram each map something real — as we understand them: personality preferences, behavioral styles, core motivations. Each is a valuable lens, and each is best described in its own words.

Prism maps something that sits underneath that — how you receive and process the world from the inside. Your cognitive rhythm. Your sensory patterns. The things that shape every experience before you've decided how to respond to it.

Prism gives you language — for things you've always sensed about yourself but never had words for. First, a map that helps you understand and trust your own experience. Then, if you choose, one you can share with the people who matter — and say: this is how I work.

Myers-Briggs — personality type DISC — behavioral style Enneagram — core motivation Prism — how you receive the world
